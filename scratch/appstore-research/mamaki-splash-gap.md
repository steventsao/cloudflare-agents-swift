## What Splash Jr Is Really Selling

Splash Jr is not presenting itself as a single toddler game. The listing positions it as a preschool-readiness platform for ages 1-4 with breadth, curriculum order, parent trust, and subscription depth.

Current App Store signals:

- Title: `Splash Jr: Toddler Games 2-4`
- Subtitle: `Learn ABCs, Colors & Shapes`
- Category: Education, with Family/Games/Puzzle genres.
- Rating: about 4.5 from 1.8K ratings.
- Promise: preschool readiness for ages 1-4, backed by a claim of 60M learners.
- Monetization: free with in-app purchases, including monthly/yearly memberships.

## Screenshot Pattern

Splash's nine iPad screenshots follow a clear sales sequence:

1. Broad category promise: `Toddler learning games`, `Ages 1+`, `60M Learners`.
2. Single concrete activity: coloring + phonics, `Color, create, learn`.
3. Single concrete skill: numbers/counting.
4. Breadth proof: `4,000+ fun games` with a game-category grid.
5. Curriculum proof: `A fun path to readiness` with ordered unlocks.
6. Trust proof: `Research-backed games` with evidence badges.
7. Retention proof: `Motivation that sticks` with rewards/celebration.
8. Parent proof: `Easy progress tracking` with accuracy/completion bars.
9. Library proof: `Endless ways to play & learn` with category grid and parent entry.

The visual formula is consistent:

- 2-5 word headline at the top.
- One big proof object per frame.
- Large text, white headline, saturated kid-safe color field.
- Tablet frame as the product evidence container.
- Parent-facing evidence appears after child-facing fun, not before.

## Mamaki Gap

I could not find Mamaki assets in this repo or a public Mamaki App Store listing from quick search. This gap is therefore inferred against the Splash pattern.

Likely current gap:

- Product framing: Mamaki needs to be framed as a learning system, not a set of one-off games.
- Breadth: Splash sells thousands of games. Mamaki can start smaller, but needs visible categories and a roadmap.
- Curriculum spine: Splash has a path with ordered skills. Mamaki needs a visible progression map.
- Parent value: Splash sells progress tracking. Mamaki needs parent-facing reports even if the first version is simple.
- Trust: Splash uses research/evidence badges. Mamaki needs a credible substitute: expert-reviewed, culturally grounded, ad-free, privacy-first, teacher/parent approved, or standards-aligned.
- Retention: Splash has rewards, levels, coins, and celebration. Mamaki needs a consistent reward language.
- App Store conversion: Mamaki screenshots should tell the same story instead of only showing gameplay.

## Structure Mamaki The Same Way

Use Splash's architecture, but own a narrower niche.

Recommended positioning:

`Mamaki: Hawaiian Learning Games`

Possible subtitle:

`Colors, Words, Nature & Shapes`

Content pillars:

- Words: Hawaiian/English vocabulary, sounds, object recognition.
- Numbers: count flowers, shells, stars, fish, fruit, leaves.
- Shapes/colors: match natural objects by color/shape.
- Creativity: coloring, tracing, sticker scenes, music/rhythm.
- Culture/nature: island animals, plants, ocean, weather, family words.
- Parent mode: weekly progress, mastered words, favorite activities.

App structure:

1. Parent setup: child name, age, language comfort, goals.
2. Child home: character/avatar, level path, today's activity.
3. Learning path: ordered islands/locations, each tied to a skill.
4. Game library: categories like Words, Counting, Shapes, Music, Coloring, Stories.
5. Reward layer: stickers, badges, scene decoration, streak-free progress.
6. Parent dashboard: minutes played, skills practiced, words learned, next recommendation.
7. Paywall: unlock full island/world, all games, offline mode, reports.

## App Store Screenshot Set For Mamaki

Mirror Splash's sequence:

1. `Hawaiian learning games` with age badge and strongest character/world art.
2. `Learn words through play` with one vocabulary interaction.
3. `Count, match, discover` with one numbers interaction.
4. `Growing game library` with 6 category tiles.
5. `A path through the islands` with a progression map.
6. `Made for early learners` with ad-free/privacy/expert/cultural trust proof.
7. `Rewards kids love` with stickers/badges/scene completion.
8. `Progress parents can see` with simple parent dashboard.
9. `Endless island play` with the larger category grid and parent mode entry.

The key is not copying Splash art. Copy the business structure: broad promise, concrete activities, breadth, curriculum path, trust, retention, parent proof, and library depth.
